Thomas J. Watson Sr.

Thomas J. Watson Sr. was born in Campbell,NewYork on February 17, 1874. He was a pioneer in the development of accounting and computing equipment used today by business, government, science and industry. He built a worldwide industry during his 42 years at IBM. In 1914 Watson became president of the Computing-Tabulating-Recording Company, a maker of electrical punch-card computing systems and other products; the company changed its name to International Business Machines Corporation in 1924. Watson was active in civic affairs and was noted for his efforts on behalf of the arts and world peace.

Mr.Watson passed away in June 19, 1956 at the age of 86 in Manhattan, New York city, U.S. He was known as one of the richest men of his time and was called the world's greatest salesman at the time of his death.

    Mr.Watsons Personal life and achievements
  1. Watson served as a powerful trustee of Columbia University from June 6, 1933, until his death.
  2. In 1937, Watson was awarded the Order of the German Eagle by Adolf Hitler.
  3. In 1939, he received an honorary degree in Doctor of Commercial Science from Oglethorpe University.
  4. He was posthumously inducted into the Junior Achievement U.S. Business Hall of Fame in 1990.
